There is a quiet revolution happening in the infrastructure layer of the web.
After a decade of JavaScript sprawl — single-page applications, hydration waterfalls,
and megabyte bundles shipped to render a blog post — engineers are rediscovering
something that was always true: **the fastest page is the one already on the server**.

## What Changed

The tooling caught up with the philosophy. Static site generators matured.
Edge networks made global delivery trivial. And the costs of complexity
finally became impossible to ignore.

## Why It Matters

Signal over noise applies to bytes, too. A page that loads in 300ms
on a $5 VPS is a better product than one that requires a distributed
React runtime and three API calls to render a headline.
